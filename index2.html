What Is Inventory Ace?

▼

The One-Line Description

Inventory Ace is a cloud-based bar inventory management system that replaces spreadsheets and manual tracking with real-time visibility across all your locations.

The Problem We Solve

Bar operators juggle multiple spreadsheets, paper logs, and disconnected POS systems. They don't know their actual stock levels until they're out of something. They catch discrepancies weeks later, if ever. They spend hours on inventory counts that could be automated.

🎯 How to Pitch This

Lead with the pain, not the product.

"How do you currently track inventory across your bars? Most operators we talk to are using spreadsheets — and they're finding out they're out of vodka on a Saturday night. Inventory Ace gives you real-time stock levels across every location, so you're never caught off guard."

Key Points to Remember

☁️

Cloud-Based, Not On-Premise

Access from anywhere. No servers to maintain. Updates happen automatically.

📱

Mobile-First Design

Staff can use phones or tablets. Barcode scanning works with any camera.

🏢

Multi-Venue Ready

One dashboard to see all locations, or drill down to any single bar.

Core Features & How to Sell Them

▼

Inventory Tracking

Every product tracked across every location. Par-level alerts tell you when stock is getting low. Cycle counts reconcile physical vs. system inventory.

💬 What to Say

Focus on prevention, not just tracking.

"Instead of finding out Monday morning that you ran out of lime juice Saturday night, you'll get an alert when you hit your par level. You can reorder before the rush — not after the damage is done."

Barcode Scanning

Scan with phone camera or enter codes manually. Quick product lookup during transactions.

💬 What to Say

Remove friction for barbacks.

"Your barbacks aren't going to search through a list for 'Bacardi Superior' at 11pm on a busy night. They scan the bottle, record the transaction, done. It's faster than paper."

Transfer System

Move stock between locations with full tracking: request → ship → receive. Cancellations restore stock automatically.

💬 What to Say

Show the accountability chain.

"If your main bar needs stock from the back storage, they request a transfer. When storage ships it, stock deducts immediately. When the bar receives it, it appears. No more 'I thought we had more' or missing product with no paper trail."

AI Analytics

Sales velocity, week-over-week trends, busy hour heatmaps, 7-day demand forecasting.

💬 What to Say

Position AI as your competitive advantage.

"Our AI looks at your transaction history and tells you that you sell 40% more tequila on Friday nights after 9pm. So you know to bump up your par level before the weekend — not guess."

Staff & Shift Management

Clock in/out, break tracking, active shift dashboard, labor cost per shift.

💬 What to Say

Connect inventory to accountability.

"You can see exactly who was working when product went missing. It's not about blaming people — it's about having visibility. When staff know there's a system tracking everything, shrinkage drops."

Event Management

Create events, assign staff, track guest counts and budgets, link transactions to specific events.

💬 What to Say

Show event profitability.

"Corporate event on Friday? Link it in the system. You'll see exactly what product was used, what revenue came in, and the actual profit margin. No more guessing if private events are worth it."

Handling Objections

▼

Click each objection to reveal the response strategy.

"We're already using spreadsheets — they work fine." +

Acknowledge: "Spreadsheets do work, and many operators are successful with them. The question is: at what cost?"

Then pivot: "How many hours a week does your team spend updating spreadsheets? How quickly can you see stock levels across all locations right now? With Inventory Ace, it's real-time — and your staff spend that time on service instead."

Close: "Would you trade 10 hours a week of manual entry for a system that updates itself?"

"My staff won't use it — they're resistant to new technology." +

Acknowledge: "Change is hard, especially in bars where routines are established."

Then pivot: "Inventory Ace is designed for speed, not complexity. Scan a barcode, tap a few buttons, done. It's faster than writing it down. When staff see it saves them time, resistance drops."

Evidence: "We onboard in under an hour. Your staff will be using it by end of shift."

"We already have a POS system that tracks inventory." +

Acknowledge: "POS systems do track sales, which affects inventory levels."

Then pivot: "But POS doesn't track receiving shipments, transfers between locations, comps, spills, or inventory that walks out the back door. Inventory Ace connects all those dots."

Key point: "POS tells you what sold. We tell you what happened to everything else."

"It's too expensive." +

Acknowledge: "I understand budget is a consideration."

Then pivot: "How much product do you think walks out the door unaccounted for each month? Shrinkage averages 2-3% in bars. On a $500K annual inventory, that's $10-15K you're already losing."

Close: "Inventory Ace costs X per month. You'd need to recover less than 1% of shrinkage to pay for it. What would 2% shrinkage reduction mean to your bottom line?"

"We need something simpler." +

Acknowledge: "Simple is definitely the goal."

Then pivot: "Inventory Ace is built for bars, not accountants. The most common tasks — recording transactions, checking stock, scanning barcodes — are designed to take under 10 seconds. You don't need to be tech-savvy."

Demo anchor: "Let me show you — I'll time myself recording a transaction. Five seconds."

"What if we have multiple bars? Can it handle that?" +

Acknowledge: "That's actually our strength."

Key points:

  • See all locations in one dashboard
  • Transfer stock between any locations
  • Each bar manager sees only their location
  • Admin sees everything with aggregated reports

Close: "You can manage 2 bars or 20 from the same login."

Practice Scenarios

▼

Practice your pitch in these common sales situations.

Scenario 1 of 4 — Single Bar Owner

The Skeptical Solo Operator

You meet a bar owner who runs a single 6-bar pub. He's been in business 15 years, does inventory once a month with pen and paper. He says his system works and doesn't see the point of changing.

Approach: Don't sell features. Sell time and peace of mind.

Key points:

  • 15 years of experience is valuable — respect it
  • Ask: "What happens when you're out of something unexpected?"
  • Offer a free trial so he can see with his own data
  • Focus on the monthly count: "What if you never had to do that count manually again?"
Scenario 2 of 4 — Multi-Venue Manager

The Overwhelmed Multi-Venue Manager

You talk to someone managing 3 bars. They have different managers at each location who all do things differently. Corporate has no visibility into what's actually happening. They're always firefighting.

Approach: Sell centralized control and accountability.

Key points:

  • One dashboard for all 3 locations
  • Each manager has their own login and responsibilities
  • Corporate sees aggregated AND per-location data
  • Transfers between locations are tracked automatically
  • Alerts go to the right manager AND corporate

Demo tip: Show the multi-venue dashboard view first.

Scenario 3 of 4 — High-Volume Nightclub

The High-Volume Club Owner

Club owner with 500+ capacity. Says they already do POS integrated inventory and that's sufficient. High volume, fast pace, staff turn over frequently.

Approach: Sell shrinkage control and staff accountability.

Key points:

  • High volume = high shrinkage risk
  • POS only tracks what scanned through register
  • What about product given away? Spills not recorded? Back door losses?
  • Barcode scan takes 3 seconds — works at speed
  • Link transactions to staff — accountability reduces shrinkage

Stat to mention: Bars lose 2-4% of inventory to shrinkage. On $2M inventory, that's $40-80K.

Scenario 4 of 4 — Event Venue

The Event Venue Coordinator

Wedding and corporate event venue with a built-in bar. Events are booked months out but they never know exact staffing needs or how much product to stock. Want to understand event profitability.

Approach: Sell event profitability tracking.

Key points:

  • Create event in system with expected guest count and budget
  • Assign staff to the event
  • Link all bar transactions to the event
  • After event: see revenue vs. product used vs. labor cost
  • Know exactly what to stock for next similar event

Key message: "Stop guessing if events are profitable. Know it."
